Weighted doubly robust learning An uplift modeling technique for estimating mixed treatments' effect
Estimating the effect of mixed treatments is a crucial problem in causal inference. While previous studies have focused on econometric analysis, few have positioned the mixed treatment problem within the realm of causal machine learning, particularly in uplift modeling. This study proposes a novel uplift modeling technique called weighted doubly robust learning, which uses Shapley-value treatment attribution and doubly robust estimation to control for confounding among different treatments and estimate the pure effect for each treatment. Experiments are conducted on both synthetic dataset and industrial dataset. The results show that our method outperforms most of the current uplift modeling approaches in responsive customer targeting and effective treatment attribution, achieving an area under uplift curve (AUUC) of 0.590 and a Qini-coefficient of 0.080. Our method not only contributes to advancing current causal machine learning methods, but also provides valuable insights for companies in business decision making.
Baoqiang Zhan, Chao Liu （刘 超）, Yongli LI, Chong Wu

How do Different Types of User-Generated Content Attract Travelers Taking Story and Review on Airbnb as the Example
User-generated content (UGC) has been validated as an important factor in travel decisions. However, it is still unclear whether different types of UGC function differently. Social cognitive theory was applied to investigate the different effects of two types of UGC, Story and Review on Airbnb, on attracting travelers through 2,271 accommodations in Beijing from December 2018 to September 2019 via the spatial Durbin model. The results indicate that, first, Story plays a positive direct role on reservation, implying that Story works as a push factor in attracting newcoming travelers to visit story-related accom modations; second, Review works as a pull factor by attracting extant travelers who have investigated nearby accommoda tions, based on the negative spatial spillover effect; and third, Review works as a moderating factor between Story and reservation. This research extends social cognitive theory to analyze different types of UGC functions in travel decisions.
Sihan Li, Yongli LI, Chao Liu （刘 超）, Ningyuan Fan

The Effect of Consumer Complaints on Firm Sales, New Evidence from a Public Complaint Platform
The development of platform economy promotes the rapidly widespread use of a public complaint platform in recent years. As a platform designed only for posting negative comments, its effect on firms’ sales would be different with the traditional online reviews that allow both positive and negative comments. To this end, this work aims to unveil how the public complaint platform affects the corresponding firms’ sales. Interestingly, both the feedback theory and the negative word-of-mouth theory can answer the research question, but they achieve opposite results. Then, this work designed three gradually deepening studies to determine which theory dominates based on our elaborately collected data from the Heimao platform. The first one is to design a quasi-natural experiment by regarding the platform usage as a treatment and adopt the Propensity Score Matching (PSM) and Difference-in-Differences Model (DID) to investigate which theory dominates, the second one is to reveal the mechanism of the mixed effect of the two theories and unveil how the two theories take effect, during which we developed the Long-Short-Term-Memory neural network (LSTM) and a network-based model to deal with the challenge of measuring the explanatory variables of reflecting the two theories, and the third one is to consider the firm type as the moderating variable to fulfill the results about the effect mechanism. Accordingly, we find that (1) the feedback theory dominates in our collected dataset, (2) the feedback theory takes a moderating effect and can offset the negative impact of online word-of-mouth; (3) firm type affects the strength of the moderating effect of the feedback theory. This work also contributes to providing insights on optimizing the complaint platform’s operation and the firm’s complaint management.
Chao Liu （刘 超）, Yongli LI
Referral strategy based on social network incentive
Customers’ social network is often regarded as a valuable capital for companies and individuals. Although quite a few studies have empirically demonstrated the positive influence of customers’ social networks on boosting sale performance, it is still unclear that how to utilize customers’ social network to exactly design profitable and feasible referral incentive strategies. To this end, this work establishes a deterministic game model including two kinds of market participants, one seller and a set of customers, and the targeted referral incentive strategy is modelled as one of the decision variables that will be determined via solving the game model. Besides, since it is an NP-hard problem of solving the model, this work provides an approximate algorithm to achieve a satisfactory solution. Theoretical analysis and numerical experiments are conducted to explore the properties of the model and the accuracy of the algorithm. In all, the proposed model, algorithm and findings contribute both theoretic models and practical operations in the field of social network and social computation.
Yongli LI, Chao Liu （刘 超）, Chuang Wei, Xiaochen Ma

Value measurement model of social network and its application in recommendation strategy
Social networks constitute the skeleton of social and economic life. Under different management scenarios, social networks in broad sense are given different forms such as friend networks, recommendation networks or loan networks. A large number of empirical studies have confirmed the significant influence of social networks in various specific scenarios of economic and social life, but few studies have quantified the role of such significant influence, especially put forward the general principle and method framework of social network value measurement. The Research on the Value Measurement Model of Social Network and Its Application in Recommendation Strategies establishes a general model framework to measure the value of social network based on the monopoly market by considering the changes in the welfare level of market participants caused by network effects. Based on this framework, the values of “nodes”, “links” and “structures”, which are the elements of the network, are measured respectively, and the analytic solutions of the value measurement of various elements are given, the relevant attribute principles are deduced and the relevant laws affecting the value of various elements are explored. In different market scenarios, the book focuses on the application of theoretical models in the design and implementation of recommendation strategies, which has direct practical significance for the management of real scenarios.
Yongli LI, Lixin Qiao, Chao Liu （刘 超）, Ningyuan Fan
Value measurement model of network externality in a crowdfunding platform
In the era of “Internet +”, crowdfunding has gradually become a new mode of e-commerce. Starting from the feature analysis of the crowdfunding pattern, this paper takes the characteristics of network externality and the structure of perfect monopoly market as the breakthrough point, then proposes the value measurement model of network externality of crowdfunding platform. This model is based on the crowdfunding initiator’s revenue function and the consumer utility function which considers the macro and micro network externality. By establishing a two-stage game model, the optimal price vectors of crowdfunding initiators, the optimal purchase quantity of participants and the optimal income level of crowdfunding initiators are solved respectively in the cases of differential pricing strategy, single uniform price strategy and benchmark model without network externality. Then the strategic value and intrinsic value of network externality of crowdfunding platform are obtained by comparing the optimal returns of crowdfunding initiators using different strategies. In addition, the theoretical results of the model are applied to the special structure of referral network, and a crowdfunding platform with recommendation function is designed. These results show that the designed crowdfunding platform with the recommendation function in this paper can encourage participants to recommend more people to join in the crowdfunding project, and can greatly raise the initiator’s income level. To sum up, the proposed model and method is significant for crowdfunding initiators to raise their income levels so as to be meaningful in improving the marketing modes in real crowdfunding platforms.
Yongli LI, Chao Liu （刘 超）, Ningyuan Fan, Zhiping Fan
